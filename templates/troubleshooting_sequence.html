Sequence Number Issues

Diagnose and recover from FIX sequence number problems without disrupting live sessions.
Critical Warning
Never reset sequence numbers while orders are live
Quick Triage
  • 1 Check the error message — does it say MsgSeqNum too low or MsgSeqNum too high? The direction tells you which side is out of sync.
  • 2 Note the expected vs received sequence numbers from the reject message — Tag 45 (RefSeqNum) and Tag 58 (Text) contain the expected and received values.
  • 3 Check your outbound sequence counter — does it match what the counterparty expects? A stale or corrupted sequence store after a restart is the most common cause.
  • 4 Check your inbound sequence counter — are you missing messages from the counterparty? If your counter is behind theirs, you need to send a ResendRequest.
  • 5 Determine if the gap occurred on your side, their side, or both — this determines whether you send a ResendRequest, respond to theirs, or need a coordinated reset.
  • 6 Decide: ResendRequest, GapFill, or Hard Reset — see Recovery Basics below. Use the least disruptive option first.
Common Problems
Symptom Likely Cause What to Check
MsgSeqNum too low Sequence not reset after previous session reset Your outbound counter vs counterparty's expected value
MsgSeqNum too high Missed inbound messages Send ResendRequest for the missing range
Sequence gap on reconnect Abrupt disconnect without Logout Reconnect without ResetSeqNumFlag, then send ResendRequest
Duplicate sequence numbers Counter reset mid-session Audit your sequence storage — file or database
Counterparty sends ResendRequest You missed their message Retransmit with PossDupFlag=Y
Both sides out of sync Crash or hard restart Coordinate reset with counterparty, confirm no open orders
Recovery Basics

Three tools to recover sequence numbers. Use the least disruptive option first.

Method When to Use Risk
ResendRequest 35=2 Gap in inbound messages Low — no disruption to outbound
GapFill 35=4, GapFillFlag=Y Skip administrative messages during resend Low — standard FIX behavior
Hard Reset ResetSeqNumFlag=Y Both sides confirmed out of sync, no open orders High — must coordinate with counterparty

Example ResendRequest — Tag 7 (BeginSeqNo) is the first missing message; Tag 16 (EndSeqNo) = 0 means through the last sent.

8=FIX.4.2|9=65|35=2|49=CLIENTA|56=BROKRB|34=8|52=20240315-09:35:00|7=5|16=0|10=xxx|
Reading the Logs
ERROR: Received MsgSeqNum 47, expected 43 INFO: Sending ResendRequest BeginSeqNo=43 EndSeqNo=0 INFO: Received Resend: MsgSeqNum=43 PossDupFlag=Y INFO: Received Resend: MsgSeqNum=44 PossDupFlag=Y INFO: Received GapFill: NewSeqNo=47 INFO: Session synchronized. Next expected: 47
  • • The gap was inbound — messages 43–46 were missed, likely due to a network blip. Your engine sent a ResendRequest for the range automatically.
  • • PossDupFlag=Y on retransmitted messages — process them but check ExecID (17) for duplicates before applying any fills to your position.
  • • The GapFill skipped messages 45–46 — the counterparty is telling you those were administrative messages (heartbeats, test requests) that you do not need.
Before You Reset

Run through this before touching any sequence numbers. A 5-minute check here prevents hours of reconciliation later.

Sequence Numbers — Pre-Reset Checklist
5-Minute Diagnosis
Sequence Numbers — TL;DR
  1. Is the error MsgSeqNum too low or too high?
  2. What sequence numbers does each side expect?
  3. Is the gap on your inbound, your outbound, or both?
  4. Can ResendRequest (35=2) or GapFill recover — or is a reset required?
  5. Reconcile open orders before changing any sequence state.
