Duplicate Orders
Identify whether an order was actually submitted more than once, determine where duplication occurred, and prevent a resend or recovery event from creating unintended exposure.
Critical Warning
Do not cancel or resend anything until you confirm which order states actually exist.

A suspected duplicate may be:

  • two genuine New Order Single messages
  • one order processed twice internally
  • a resent message with PossDupFlag (43) = Y
  • a duplicate ClOrdID (11)
  • two different ClOrdIDs representing the same business instruction
  • an order visible in one system but not another

Before taking action:

Quick Triage
  • 1 Confirm whether one or two New Order Single messages were sent. Pull the outbound session log and count the outbound 35=D messages for the affected ClOrdID.
  • 2 Compare ClOrdID (11) values. Identical ClOrdIDs on two outbound messages confirm a duplicate submission. Different ClOrdIDs mean a duplicate business decision, not a FIX-level duplicate.
  • 3 Compare exchange or broker OrderID (37) values. Two distinct OrderIDs confirm both orders were accepted. One OrderID means only one reached the exchange.
  • 4 Check PossDupFlag (43) and OrigSendingTime (122). A resent message with 43=Y should be deduplicated by the counterparty — but verify whether your application treated it as a new order.
  • 5 Review Resend Requests and session recovery. A session gap followed by a Resend Request is the most common trigger for unintended duplicate submissions.
  • 6 Confirm current open and filled quantity before canceling anything. Canceling the wrong order, or canceling when only one order is live, can leave you flat or over-exposed.

Key Fields

Pull these from both the outbound messages and inbound Execution Reports before drawing any conclusions.

Tag Purpose

Reading the Logs

Example 1 — Same ClOrdID submitted twice:

OUT 35=D|11=ABC123|55=AAPL|54=1|38=100| OUT 35=D|11=ABC123|55=AAPL|54=1|38=100|
  • • The same ClOrdID was submitted twice — this is a FIX-level duplicate.
  • • Determine whether the second message was rejected or accepted — check the inbound Execution Reports for each.
  • • Do not assume the broker ignored it — some venues accept both and assign a distinct OrderID to each.

Example 2 — Resent message with PossDupFlag:

OUT 35=D|11=ABC123|43=Y|122=20260714-13:30:00.100|
  • • PossDupFlag (43) = Y signals this message was resent — the counterparty is expected to deduplicate it.
  • • Verify whether the application treated the resent message as a new order submission rather than a retransmission.
  • • OrigSendingTime (122) reflects the original transmission — not the time of the resend.

5-Minute Diagnosis
Duplicate Orders — TL;DR
  1. Was the same ClOrdID submitted twice, or are there two different ClOrdIDs?
  2. How many distinct OrderID values did the counterparty return?
  3. Was PossDupFlag (43) = Y present — and did the application treat it correctly?
  4. Was this triggered by session recovery or a Resend Request?
  5. What is the total open quantity across all suspected live orders?
